# Managing Stock

Stock management is critical to running a successful store. If your stock numbers are wrong, customers might order products that you do not actually have, leading to cancellations, delays, and unhappy customers. The **Stock** page gives you a centralized place to monitor inventory levels across all your products and update stock quantities quickly.

---

## What Is Stock Management?

Stock (also called inventory) refers to the **number of units you have available to sell** for each product or variant. When a customer places an order, the stock count automatically decreases by the quantity they purchased. When stock reaches **zero**, the product (or variant) is marked as **"Out of Stock"** and customers can no longer purchase it.

Proper stock management means:
- Always knowing how many units you have of each product
- Restocking before items run out
- Avoiding overselling (selling more than you actually have)

## Updating Stock Quantities

To update the stock for a product or variant:

1. **Expand** the product by clicking on it
2. You will see the current stock displayed in a **number field** between two buttons
3. You have three ways to adjust the stock:

### Method 1: Use the Plus/Minus Buttons

- Click the **minus button** (`âˆ’`) on the left to decrease the stock by 1
- Click the **plus button** (`+`) on the right to increase the stock by 1
- Each click changes the value by exactly 1 unit

### Method 2: Type the Number Directly

- Click on the **number field** in the center
- Delete the current number and type the new stock quantity
- This is faster when you need to make large adjustments (e.g., changing stock from 4 to 50)

### Method 3: Combine Both

- Use the buttons for small adjustments
- Type directly for large changes

After adjusting the number:

4. Click the **Save** button (black button next to the controls)
5. The stock is updated immediately

## Understanding How Stock Changes Automatically

Stock numbers change automatically in several situations:

| Event | What Happens to Stock |
|---|---|
| **Customer places an order** | Stock decreases by the quantity ordered |
| **Order is cancelled and deleted** | Stock is restored (increased back by the quantity that was ordered) |
| **You manually update stock** | Stock changes to whatever value you set |

### Automatic Decrease on Purchase

When a customer buys 2 units of a product (or variant), the stock automatically decreases by 2. You do not need to manually adjust it after each sale.

### Stock Restoration on Cancelled Orders

When an admin deletes a **cancelled** order from the Sales page, the system automatically **restores** the stock for all products in that order. For example:

- Customer orders 2 units of "Luxury Coat (XXL Black)"
- Stock goes from 6 â†’ 4
- Order is cancelled and admin deletes it
- Stock is restored from 4 â†’ 6

This ensures your inventory stays accurate even when orders are cancelled.

## Stock Management Best Practices

### Set Accurate Initial Stock

When you first add a product, make sure the stock number matches your **actual physical inventory**. Count your items before entering the number. An incorrect starting stock leads to problems later.

### Regular Stock Audits

At least once a month, compare your **digital stock numbers** (shown on the Stock page) with your **actual physical inventory**. If there are discrepancies, update the digital numbers to match reality.

> **Tip:** Schedule a specific day each month for stock audits. For example, the first Monday of every month. Consistency prevents stock drift from going unnoticed.

### Restock Before Running Out

Do not wait until stock reaches 0 to reorder. When you see the **Low Stock** badge, start the reordering process immediately. The time it takes to receive new inventory could mean days or weeks of lost sales.

### Keep Safety Stock

Consider maintaining a small **safety stock** (buffer) for your best-selling items. For example, if you typically sell 10 units per month, keep at least 5 extra units as buffer to handle unexpected demand spikes.

### Monitor Seasonal Demand

Certain products sell more during specific seasons:
- **Coats and jackets** â€” Higher demand in autumn and winter
- **Light shirts** â€” Higher demand in spring and summer
- **Formal suits** â€” Spikes around wedding season and festival periods

Adjust your stock levels based on upcoming seasonal demand to avoid running out during peak periods.

## Multi-Variant Stock Tips

When managing stock for multi-variant products, keep these points in mind:

> **Tip:** Popular sizes (M, L, XL) typically sell faster than extreme sizes (XS, 3XL). Stock more units of your popular sizes.

> **Tip:** If one color variant is outselling others, consider increasing its stock and reducing stock for slower-moving colors.

> **Tip:** When a specific variant runs out of stock, it does NOT affect other variants of the same product. Customers can still purchase available variants. Only the sold-out variant is marked as unavailable.

> **Tip:** Check which variants have the highest turnover rate (sales per week) and prioritize restocking those first.

## Common Stock Mistakes

| Mistake | Problem | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Setting initial stock to 0 | Product immediately shows as "Out of Stock" | Set accurate stock when creating the product |
| Not clicking Save after adjusting | Changes are lost when you leave the page | Always click the Save button after making changes |
| Ignoring Low Stock warnings | Product runs out and customers cannot buy it | Check Low Stock badges daily and restock promptly |
| Not doing physical audits | Digital stock drifts from actual inventory | Compare digital and physical stock monthly |
| Overstocking slow-moving items | Capital tied up in unsold inventory | Order conservatively for new products and increase based on demand |

## Troubleshooting

### Stock Shows Wrong Number

If the stock number does not match your actual inventory:
1. Navigate to the Stock page
2. Find the product and expand it
3. Type the **correct** number in the stock field
4. Click **Save**
5. Consider doing a full inventory audit to check other products

### Product Shows "Out of Stock" But You Have Inventory

This means the digital stock is 0 even though you physically have units:
1. Go to the Stock page
2. Find the product (look for the **Out of Stock** red badge)
3. Expand it and update the stock to the correct number
4. Click **Save**
5. The product will immediately become available for purchase again

### Stock Did Not Decrease After a Sale

This is rare but can happen if there is a system issue:
1. Check the Sales page to confirm the order was actually placed
2. Manually decrease the stock by the ordered quantity
3. If this happens repeatedly, contact your developer to investigate
